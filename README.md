## What is Build in Public?

**Build in Public** (BiP) is the practice of openly sharing your product development, business journey, or creative process in real time on platforms like X, GitHub, LinkedIn, and your own website.

Instead of hiding your work until it’s perfect, you document your progress, challenges, wins, and learnings publicly. This builds trust, attracts an audience, generates valuable feedback, and often leads to faster growth and opportunities.

### Why Build in Public Works
- Natural audience growth without paid ads
- Early validation from real people
- Stronger personal brand and community support
- Higher trust → easier to sell or collaborate
- Works for developers, designers, writers, marketers, and solo founders

## Getting Started with Build in Public

You don’t need a finished product or existing audience.

1. **Choose your project** — A side project, SaaS tool, newsletter, learning journey, or personal brand.
2. **Set a simple goal** — Example: “Share 4 updates per week” or “Launch my first version in 60 days.”
3. **Create your first public home** — A landing page or GitHub repository.
4. **Announce your intention** — Post your first update today.
5. **Stay consistent** — Consistency is the #1 key to success.

**Pro Tip**: Start small. Focus on building the habit rather than perfection.

## Share Your Journey: What to Post and How Often

Authenticity and consistency are what make Build in Public effective.

### Good things to share:
- Daily or weekly progress
- Challenges and how you solved them
- Screenshots, demos, or code snippets
- Lessons learned
- Questions for your audience

**Simple post template:**
> Day X of building [Project Name] in public.  
> Today I worked on [task].  
> The hardest part was [challenge].  
> Here’s what I learned: [lesson].  
> Next up: [plan].  
> What do you think? #buildinpublic

**Recommended frequency**: 3–7 times per week when starting.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Posting inconsistently
- Only sharing wins (people connect with real struggles)
- Not replying to comments
- Obsessing over follower count too early
- Trying to share everything and burning out
- Starting too big instead of building the habit

## FAQ

**Do I need a finished product to start?**  
No. Many people begin by sharing their learning process or early ideas.

**What if I have zero followers?**  
Everyone starts at zero. Consistent value + engagement grows your audience naturally.

**Should I share revenue?**  
It’s your choice. Many do it for transparency, but start with your process if you prefer.

**How often should I post?**  
3–7 times per week is a good starting point.

**Is Build in Public only for developers?**  
No — it works for any creator building something.
